Floods

Floods are when a lot of water covers land that is usually dry. Floods happen when there's too much rain, melting snow, or even a big wave from the ocean, and the water spreads out and covers everything in its path. This can be dangerous because it can damage homes, roads, and make it hard for people and animals to move around safely. So, floods are like when the land gets too wet and filled with water, causing problems for the things and people that live there.

Causes of Floods
  • Prolonged and intense rainfall, especially in a short period, can overwhelm rivers, streams, and drainage systems, leading to rapid runoff and flooding.
  • When accumulated snow and ice melt rapidly, such as during warm weather or due to a sudden temperature increase or rain, it can contribute to the overflow of rivers and streams, causing snowmelt-induced flooding.
  • Coastal areas can experience flooding from storm surges during hurricanes, typhoons, or tropical cyclones. These storms bring strong winds that push seawater onto the land, causing coastal inundation.
  • Tsunamis are giant waves caused by earthquakes or volcanic eruptions.
  • Glacial lakes in mountainous regions can experience outbursts when the glacier holding back the water melts or ruptures, releasing a massive flood downstream.
  • Dam or levee failures can release large volumes of water into nearby communities.

Floods Prevention

  • Improving drainage systems can help to remove excess water from communities and reduce the risk of flooding.
  • Plant trees and vegetation in watersheds and flood-prone areas to stabilize soil, reduce erosion, and enhance water absorption.
  • Dams and levees can help to control the flow of water and prevent rivers and streams from overflowing their banks.
  • Implement zoning regulations that restrict or control development in flood-prone areas.
  • Prohibit construction in high-risk flood zones.
  • Encourage the creation of green spaces and parks in flood-prone areas that can absorb excess water.
  • Develop community-based flood resilience plans that involve residents, businesses, and local governments in flood risk reduction efforts.

About Us
